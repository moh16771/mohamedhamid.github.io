Secrets of the Deep Space

The universe contains everything that exists: all matter, energy, time, and space. It is so vast that light from the most distant objects has been traveling for over 13 billion years to reach us.

Black Holes & Dark Matter

Beyond what we can see, the universe is filled with mysteries. Black holes are regions where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape. Meanwhile, dark matter and dark energy make up about 95% of the universe, yet they remain invisible to our telescopes.

Why We Explore

Exploration is in our DNA. By studying the stars, we learn about the chemical elements that make up our own bodies—as Carl Sagan said, "We are made of star-stuff."
